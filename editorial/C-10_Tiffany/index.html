The Story

A Collaborative Masterpiece: Jeremy Pierce's 1965 Chevy C-10 pick-up truck, crafted by JRW Rods and Customs.

Nestled in the picturesque town of Washington, Utah, a remarkable 1965 Chevy C-10 pick-up truck reigns supreme, embodying the perfect fusion of power, style, and craftsmanship. Owned by Jeremy Pierce, this stunning vehicle stands as a testament to the collaborative effort between Jeremy and Wayne Glasgow, a skilled Project Manager and the owner of JRW Rods and Customs.

The journey began when Jeremy stumbled upon the truck, originally a long bed, in Idaho and decided to turn his vision into a reality—an awe-inspiring, “bad-ass” custom truck that delivers exceptional performance, power, and reliability.

Throughout the project, inspiration and design cues echoed through the details — from the custom grille and air filter cover, to interior vents and finishing touches. Every component was chosen and executed to unify the truck’s overall aesthetic and showcase what’s possible when resolute dedication meets craftsmanship.

As with any ambitious project, challenges surfaced — rare parts, technical hurdles, and the constant push to avoid compromise. But obstacle-by-obstacle, the build evolved into something more than a restoration or a modification: it became a complete, modern driving experience wrapped in a classic shape.

The result is a truck that blends power with precision, and presence with purpose — a true reflection of the owner’s vision and the builder’s relentless pursuit of excellence.

Build Highlights

  • Power: GM Performance LT4 Connect & Cruise
  • Transmission: T56 6-speed manual
  • Chassis: Speedtech Performance ExtReme C-10 (Torque Arm Rear)
  • Rear: Ford-style 9"
  • Finish: Custom PPG teal + charcoal stripe
  • Interior: Modified stock dash + Dakota Digital gauges + leather upholstery
  • Details: Hidden service compartments (brake + supercharger coolant), pop-up fuel filler
