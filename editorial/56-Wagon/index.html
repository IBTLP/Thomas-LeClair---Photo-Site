1956 Belair Wagon Feature

A classic silhouette with modern confidence — built to roam, finished to stop you in your tracks.

Vehicle: 1956 Chevrolet Belair Wagon Build: JRW Rods & Customs Photography: Thomas LeClair
Download the PDF Jump to photos

There’s something almost cinematic about a wagon done right — the kind of car that feels equally at home idling at a curb in the last light of day or chewing up miles with the stereo low and the windows cracked. This 1956 Belair Wagon captures that feeling perfectly: timeless proportions, modern drivability, and detail work that rewards a closer look.

At first glance, it’s pure mid-century Chevrolet. But spend a minute with it and you start noticing the tells: the stance is too perfect, the reflections too clean, the panel fit too precise. It’s the kind of build where nothing is accidental — and nothing is left “good enough.”

“A modern grand tourer wrapped in classic lines — built for real road time, not just show lights.”

Built to drive

The goal wasn’t just to restore a wagon — it was to create something you can trust for long-distance travel. Modern reliability, modern comfort, and a chassis/powertrain package that behaves like a new car without losing the character that makes a ’56 special.

Details that matter

From the wood-toned cargo floor and clean interior stitching to the subtle hardware choices throughout, it’s a build that feels cohesive. Every part looks like it belongs in the same story — which is how you know the vision stayed intact from start to finish.

The payoff

As this luxury grand tourer prepares to embark on its maiden voyage, the countless hours of dedication culminate in modern driving character and reliability. Tim and Cheryl — with Sheila, their faithful Corgi Shepard Mix — are set to take the long way home and make real memories behind the wheel.

The 1956 Belair Wagon, meticulously customized by the craftsmen at JRW Rods & Customs, turns the open road into an infinite canvas. It’s equal parts performance and presence — a reminder that the best builds don’t just look good. They get used.

Build Notes

  • Chassis: ExtReme Chassis full chassis (modern foundation)
  • Powertrain: GM Performance LS3 Connect & Cruise package (reliability-first)
  • Transmission: 6L80 automatic
  • Rear: Strange 9" with 3.55 gears
  • Wheels: American Muscle “55” wheels
  • Interior: Hand-built, custom stitched finish with modern touches
  • Intent: A true driver — road trips encouraged
